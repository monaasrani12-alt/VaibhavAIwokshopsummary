Core Framework

The 5 Layers of Context

The single most important concept in the workshop. Every great AI output starts with giving the model all five layers of context. Miss one and the quality drops noticeably.

  1. Identity Context — Who is the AI acting as?
    Define the role, expertise level, and tone. "You are an expert financial analyst who writes for senior executives."
  2. World Context — What does the AI need to know about your situation?
    Describe your audience, business, and the environment the output will live in.
  3. Task Context — What exactly needs to happen?
    Be specific. Not "write an email" but "write a 150-word email inviting my 10K subscribers to a free live workshop."
  4. Example Context — What does great output look like? What does bad output look like?
    Showing both good and bad examples is the single biggest upgrade to output quality.
  5. Constraint Context — What are the rules?
    Word limit, tone restrictions, things to avoid, format requirements. Make them explicit.

Prompt Engineering

The Master Prompt Template

This structure, combining all 5 layers, is the template Vaibhav uses for every AI task. Copy it, fill in the brackets, and use it with any model.

[Identity Context] You are a [ROLE] who [EXPERTISE/STYLE]. [World Context] My [AUDIENCE] are [DESCRIPTION]. They [PAIN POINT / DESIRE]. [Task Context] [SPECIFIC TASK — be precise and detailed]. [Example Context] Good output looks like: [EXAMPLE OF STRONG OUTPUT]. Bad output looks like: [EXAMPLE OF WEAK OUTPUT TO AVOID]. [Constraint Context] Under [WORD COUNT] words. [TONE]. [FORMAT RULES]. No [THINGS TO EXCLUDE].

Key Principles

  • The more specific your context, the closer the AI gets to what you actually need on the first attempt.
  • Example Context is the most underused layer. Showing bad examples is just as valuable as showing good ones.
  • Save your best prompts as Claude Skills so you never have to rewrite them — just trigger them with a short command.
  • Vague prompts produce vague outputs. Treat writing a prompt like writing a brief to a talented contractor.

Google Tool

NotebookLM — Research Assistant

NotebookLM lets you upload any source — PDFs, websites, YouTube videos, Google Drive docs, audio files — and then chat with all of them at once. Every answer is cited back to your sources.

What It Can Generate From Your Sources

  • Audio Overview — a conversational podcast-style summary, useful for listening while commuting
  • Slide Deck — auto-generated presentation from your uploaded material
  • Mind Map — visual overview of concepts and connections across sources
  • Reports and Flashcards — structured summaries or study cards
  • Quiz — auto-generated questions to test your understanding
  • Infographic and Data Table — visual formats from research data

How Vaibhav Used It Live

Uploaded multiple sources covering AI and machine learning concepts. Used the Chat panel to ask "Explain the step-by-step process of building a neural network" — got a structured, multi-source cited answer in seconds. Used the Studio panel to generate an Audio Overview and a Slide Deck from the same sources without any additional prompting.

Perplexity — The Research Engine

Perplexity searches the live web first, then synthesises the findings into a structured response. Every claim is cited. This makes it far more reliable than a standard AI model for research tasks where factual accuracy matters.

What Vaibhav Demonstrated

He asked Perplexity to produce a full market entry strategy report for Outskill in Latin America. The output was a consulting-grade document with nine structured sections — Executive Summary, Market Landscape, Competitive Analysis, Country Prioritisation, Pricing Strategy, Go-to-Market Plan, SWOT Analysis, Risk Analysis, and Strategic Recommendations. Generated in under two minutes. Downloadable as PDF.

When to Use Perplexity

  • Any research task where you need cited, verifiable sources
  • Competitor analysis and market landscape mapping
  • Generating structured reports you can share with others
  • Staying current with fast-moving news or industry trends
  • When you need to be confident your AI output is grounded in real data

Claude Advanced Features

Claude Skills & Cowork

Two features of Claude that Vaibhav demonstrated go significantly beyond basic chat — Skills for saving reusable workflows, and Cowork Mode for complex multi-step tasks.

Claude Skills

A Skill is a saved prompt workflow. You define it once — with all 5 layers of context, your preferred output format, tone guidelines, and constraints — and Claude remembers it. Next time you need the same task done, you reference the skill and Claude applies all your rules automatically without you retyping anything.

Vaibhav demonstrated creating a "Newsletter Email Skill" live in Claude Code. The skill defined his writing style, audience profile, what good and bad emails look like, and format rules. He then used it to generate a breaking news email about OpenAI's upcoming "Spud" model — Claude searched the web for current information and applied the skill format in one step.

Cowork Mode

Claude's agentic capability. In Cowork Mode, Claude can read files, search the web, run code, save outputs to Google Drive, and complete multi-step tasks autonomously. Vaibhav used it to build a full AI Implementation Roadmap presentation by feeding it 30+ podcast episodes — it synthesised the content and produced a formatted five-slide PPTX deck saved directly to his Drive.

What is an MCP?
Model Context Protocol — a standard that lets AI models read from and write to external apps directly, without manual copy-pasting.
Apps connectable via MCP:
Gmail Calendar Google Drive Slack Notion Asana Salesforce GitHub
Once connected, the AI can act on these apps autonomously — reading, writing, and updating without you intervening.

Summary

Key Takeaways

1
The 5 Layers of Context is the most important framework from the entire workshop. Use all five every time you prompt any AI model. The more context you provide, the better the output on the first attempt.
2
Claude is the preferred model for serious, professional-grade outputs. Use GPT-5 for speed and creativity. Use Gemini for research and Google integration. Use Claude when quality and depth matter most.
3
Save your best workflows as Claude Skills and never rewrite them. One-word triggers activate your full prompt, tone guidelines, and format rules. This is where the 2-hour daily saving compounds over time.
4
NotebookLM and Perplexity replace hours of manual research. Upload your documents to NotebookLM to chat with them. Use Perplexity when you need cited, real-time research and structured reports.
5
You do not need to write code to build functional applications. Stitch, Bolt, and Lovable can produce complete websites and apps from a single descriptive prompt.
6
MCPs transform AI from a chat tool into an autonomous operator. Connect Claude to your Gmail, Drive, calendar, and CRM — then let it execute multi-step tasks without manual intervention.
7
Consistent daily practice matters more than any single session. "The gap between people who use AI consistently and those who don't is growing every month." — Vaibhav Sisinty
